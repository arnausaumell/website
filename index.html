Research Experience

I'm interested in machine learning, deep learning, image processing and computer vision. Thus, much of my research experience is dedicated to these fields.

Visiting Student at MIT

September 2022 - February 2023

Currently performing my Bachelor's Final Thesis at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), under the advice of Prof. Alberto Rodriguez. Developing a project on deformable object perception and manipulation through vision and tactile feedback, with a special focus on perception from an task-agnostic point of view.

Deep Learning Intern at Starlab-Neuroscience

June 2021 - September 2021

Developed LSTM Denoising Autoencoders for neurological signal (EEG) processing. Constructed a pipeline for EEG artefact detection and correction. As a side project, performed an exploratory study of the latent space generated by the model, coming up with an interesting wavelet decomposition of the signals and a nice approach to perform bilinear interpolation between different EEGs. Altogether with the previous work lead to a first-author abstract publication and poster presentation at the 2022 INCF Assembly.

Machine Learning Intern at Institute of Robotics and Informatics (IRI)

September 2020 - June 2021

Developed a project within the TASC module (Topological Analysis of Simulated Cloth manipulations). Implemented a new approach to enable a stratification of all the possible configurations of a rectangular mesh over a plain surface, based on a new topological approach of the surface. Using such a representation, clustered top-head images of the mesh into different fold categories, specially focusing on how the stratification approach affected the clusters formation.

Research Intern at Institute of Photonic Sciences (ICFO)

September 2017 - June 2017

Collaboration in the Theoretical Quantum-Nano Photonics group. Focused on the development of a Fourier-transformation-based algorithm for image compression.
